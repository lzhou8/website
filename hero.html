H.E.R.O for Children: Database Management System

Introduction:

H.E.R.O for Children is a nonprofit organization with the mission of supporting QOL (quality of life) for children with HIV and AIDs.
As a past member of Epic Intentions (an organization that empowers students to develop software for nonprofits), I had the opportunity of working on a project for H.E.R.O for Children in Spring 2017.

Contributions:

From January to May 2017, I worked in an interdisplinary team of 6 Georgia Tech students to develop a database management system (DBMS) for H.E.R.O. for Children, Inc. Within the team, I contributed to front-end development in Python and Tkinter.

Impact:

This system enables the nonprofit to manage its information through a UI (Python, Tkinter) and searchable database (MySQL). Previously, this information was stored in Excel spreadsheets, but with this project, we have provided the nonprofit with a more efficient solution to maintain records of children with HIV and AIDS and to ultimately apply for critical grants.
